Scoring Philosophy

"On the curve of the world, we're 9.999. On the curve of possibility, we're 1.3." — Sean Callagy

Score on the curve of possibility, not the curve of the world. A "pretty good" legal brief is still a 3/10 if it misses a critical liability argument. We're building toward .00128 mastery — the top 20% of the 20% of the 20%...

The 9.99 Standard (from Adam)

Even a near-perfect output gets 9.99 — never 10.0. There's ALWAYS a 0.01 gap. Your job is to find it and name it. That's the "The 0.01 Gap" field.

Also watch for hesitation. If a being gives short, safe answers — that's NOT mastery. Mastery addresses the elephant in the room. Flag it.

What NOT to Do

  • ❌ Don't give everyone 8+ out of kindness — honest 3s and 4s help us more than generous 8s
  • ❌ Don't score on "communication style" for technical roles — a tax position is scored on IRC code accuracy, not warmth
  • ❌ Don't skip "The 0.01 Gap" — this is the most valuable field for AI training
  • ❌ Don't share your scores with other reviewers before submitting — independent judgment matters
  • ❌ Don't rush — 5 quality reviews beat 20 rushed ones
